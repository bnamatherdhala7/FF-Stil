# Stil — Competitive Landscape

**Last updated:** April 2026

---

## The core gap no one has filled

Every tool in this space does one of three things:

1. **Learns your style** but requires Lightroom, 2,500+ past edits, and serves photographers only (Imagen AI, Aftershoot)
2. **Holds brand memory** but costs $49–$500+/month, targets enterprise teams, and is text/copy focused (Typeface, Jasper)
3. **Is conversational** but forgets everything the moment you close the tab (ChatGPT, Claude)

**No tool combines:** persistent cross-session style memory + conversational interface + accessible individual pricing + works from day one without a library of past edits.

That is the gap Stil occupies.

---

## Competitor breakdown

### Imagen AI
**What it is:** AI photo editing that learns a photographer's editing style from their Lightroom history.

**Pricing:** $0.05/photo (min $7/month), annual volume tiers at 10–20% discount

**What it does well:**
- Genuinely learns individual editing style
- Very accurate at replicating Lightroom adjustments
- Integrates directly into photographer workflows

**What it misses:**
- Requires Lightroom — locked to one tool
- Needs 2,500–5,000 past edits to train the model (useless for new users)
- Zero conversational interface — batch process only
- Serves professional photographers, not social media content creators
- No cross-platform style portability

**Verdict for Stil:** Not competing for the same user. A photographer using Imagen to cull wedding shoots has a completely different workflow from a creator posting 5x/week to Instagram.

---

### Aftershoot
**What it is:** AI culling and editing for photographers, built around custom AI profiles.

**Pricing:**
- Basic: $24/month (pre-built styles only)
- Pro: $40/month (1 custom profile)
- Max: $60/month (5 custom profiles, team access)

**What it does well:**
- Custom AI editing profiles trained on your actual edits
- Strong integration with Lightroom and Capture One
- Culling + editing in one workflow

**What it misses:**
- Same training data requirement: 2,500–5,000 images minimum
- Requires existing editing software (not standalone)
- No conversational interface
- Professional photographer audience, not content creators
- Style is locked to their platform

**Verdict for Stil:** Same limitation as Imagen AI — wrong audience, wrong interface, wrong pricing model for individual creators.

---

### Adobe Firefly + Creative Cloud
**What it is:** Adobe's generative AI layer across Photoshop, Illustrator, and the broader CC suite.

**Pricing:** Included in Creative Cloud ($55–$85/month) or Firefly standalone plans from $9.99/month

**What it does well:**
- "Style Kits" — upload 20 brand images and Firefly fine-tunes to match your aesthetic
- Deep Photoshop integration (Generative Fill, Generative Expand)
- Firefly Boards for team creative iteration
- Image Model 5 (2025): photorealistic 4MP generation with layered editing

**What it misses:**
- Style Kits require a library of 20 brand images to set up — no learning from usage
- Locked inside Adobe ecosystem — style doesn't travel to Canva, CapCut, etc.
- No conversational editing — every edit is a manual action
- No persistent session memory — starts fresh every time
- High cost for individual creators; CC subscription required

**Verdict for Stil:** Adobe is the incumbent for professional creative work. Stil is not trying to replace Photoshop. The opportunity is the layer above Adobe: a memory and intelligence system that knows your style and can inform any tool, including Photoshop.

---

### Canva Magic Studio
**What it is:** Canva's AI suite inside the world's most popular design platform.

**Pricing:** Free (limited) to $15–$17/month (Pro), Teams from $30/month

**What it does well:**
- "Brand Guardrails" — prevents off-brand font/colour usage
- Brand Kit stores logos, colours, fonts
- Magic Design for Video, Magic Charts
- Visual Suite 2.0 (2025): unified interface across formats
- Massive user base — 150M+ monthly active users

**What it misses:**
- Brand Kit is static setup, not learned from usage
- No memory of how you actually edit — only what you pre-define
- Heavily template-driven — outputs look like Canva
- No conversational interface for describing creative intent
- Brand memory locked inside Canva — doesn't apply to other tools

**Verdict for Stil:** Canva is the distribution platform of choice for creators. Stil complements it: the style intelligence layer that knows what to tell Canva to do.

---

### Typeface AI
**What it is:** Enterprise generative content platform with a Brand Hub that trains on company assets.

**Pricing:** Custom enterprise contracts — estimated $49–$99+/user/month; no public pricing

**What it does well:**
- Brand Hub: upload logos, colours, fonts, campaigns — AI trains on the full brand DNA
- Specialised "Arc Agents" for ideation, copy, image, and video
- Handles text + visual content in one platform

**What it misses:**
- Enterprise only — built for companies, not individual creators
- Requires a full brand asset library to set up meaningfully
- No conversational editing — workflow is template/agent based
- Complex and expensive for solo creators
- No visual photo editing features

**Verdict for Stil:** Different market entirely. Typeface is for a 50-person marketing team. Stil is for a solo creator managing their own Instagram.

---

### Jasper AI
**What it is:** AI content platform for marketing teams, starting as a copy tool and expanding to brand automation.

**Pricing:**
- Creator: $49/month (1 seat, 1 brand voice)
- Pro: $69/month (3 brand voices, team features)
- Business: Custom

**What it does well:**
- Brand Voice Memory: train once on past campaigns, tonal examples — never re-explain
- 100+ marketing-specific templates
- Multi-language, team collaboration

**What it misses:**
- Text and copy focused — limited visual editing capability
- Brand voice is language-based, not visual/aesthetic
- $49/month entry price is high for individual creators
- No photo editing, no crop/filter/export tools
- No multimodal image understanding

**Verdict for Stil:** Jasper solves the copy equivalent of what Stil solves for visuals. If anything, they're parallel products targeting different creative assets.

---

### Luminar Neo (Skylum)
**What it is:** Standalone AI photo editor for photographers and enthusiasts.

**Pricing:** Perpetual license from $99 (one-time), ecosystem/upgrade passes ~$50–$79/year

**What it does well:**
- AI sky replacement, portrait enhancement, generative erase/expand
- Intelligent editing assistant suggests adjustments per photo
- Cross-device sync (2025 update)
- Strong one-time pricing — no subscription required

**What it misses:**
- AI assistant is per-photo reactive, not style-persistent
- No cross-session memory — every photo session starts fresh
- No conversational interface
- Style presets are static, not learned from behavior
- No export preset management per platform

**Verdict for Stil:** Luminar is an excellent standalone photo editor. But it can't answer "remember that I always crop square for Instagram" — it has no concept of user-level persistent preferences.

---

### ChatGPT / Claude
**What they are:** General-purpose AI assistants with growing multimodal capability.

**What they do well:**
- Can understand creative briefs in natural language
- Can see and analyse images (GPT-4o, Claude 3.5+)
- Widely accessible, familiar interface

**What they miss:**
- Completely stateless — forget everything at session end (without explicit memory features)
- No editing tool integrations
- Cannot execute creative actions — only describe/advise
- Memory features (where available) are general purpose, not style-domain-specific

**Verdict for Stil:** ChatGPT with memory is the closest structural analogy to what Stil is building — but Stil is domain-specific (creative visual style), action-capable (runs editing tools), and has a structured style schema that tracks tone/crop/export specifically rather than general conversation history.

## The real moat

Every competitor's style memory is either:
- **Static** (Canva Brand Kit, Typeface Brand Hub — you set it up manually, it doesn't learn)
- **Tool-locked** (Imagen/Aftershoot in Lightroom, Firefly in Adobe CC)
- **Training-data-gated** (Imagen/Aftershoot need thousands of past edits)

Stil's style profile is **learned from conversation**, works **from session one**, and lives **outside any specific editing tool**. That portability is the structural differentiator that none of the above can replicate without rearchitecting their core product.

The second moat is pricing. The tools that do persistent brand memory well (Typeface, Jasper) cost $49–$500+/month and are built for teams. The tools priced for individual creators (Canva, Luminar) have no meaningful style memory. There is no product at $10–15/month that combines both.
